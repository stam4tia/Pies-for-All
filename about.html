History

Pies are thought to have originated from ancient Greece and Egypt. The ancient Greeks would eat a savory type of pie with meat inside of it, in an open shell. However, the Romans could have been the first to create a pie that included a top and bottom crust. England saw a rise in popularity for sweet pies in the 16th century. For Example, Queen Elizabeth I was known to have loved cherry pies. When the English colonized America, they brought their pie recipes with them. Pies quickly became a staple in New England households, served and enjoyed for breakfast. The Northern states became known for their pumpkin pies, the Southwest states for their variety of nut pies, the Midwest for their creamy and cheesy pies, and Florida for its key lime pies.
